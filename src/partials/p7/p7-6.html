By allowing fishermen to donate fish that otherwise must be discarded under bycatch regulations, the North Pacific Fishery Management Council unleashed a new supply of seafood for the hungry. Fishermen and processors were happy to end the once-mandatory waste of food-grade fish. SeaShare, the industry-sponsored nonprofit that pioneered this approach, reports that participants have donated more than 200 million seafood servings to foodbanks.
